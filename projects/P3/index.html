Embeddedness

 Design Authority’s body of work examines ideas of embeddedness. Our artistic pursuits inspire a look “inward”: assessing the state of the world through an analysis of the gaze of both self and other. a self-reflexive exercise in which we consider knowledgeable bounds of vision in relation to our cultures, and how this lends to further in analysis as we formate an idea of the other. We rely on this to develop creative works which express a respectable, compassionate and expanded comprehension of the diversity of cultural participation. An expansion on Anamorphosis which distinguish self and other; cultural camouflage.

Bricolage

Our postmodern reality details a specific and extensive exchange between individuals of all social, racial, sexual and religious backgrounds. This ultimately takes form as whole webs of knowledge trading across and potentially transgressing cultural bounds; in the visual arts, we use bricolage to describe the diverse range of elements for potential use in creative production, and the social dynamics which surround and construct cultural exchange.
